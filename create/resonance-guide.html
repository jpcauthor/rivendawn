I

Resonance at Creation — Checklist

Work through this checklist in order. Each item links to the full reference section below.

Shield Wardens & Forged Combatants: Resonance does not apply to your Calling. Skip this page entirely and proceed directly to Step 9 of Character Creation.
  1. Confirm your Calling type. — True Resonant, Hybrid, or Songweaver.  Resonant Types & Path Points →
  2. Record your Path Point pool. — 6 (True Resonant) · 3 (Hybrid) · 4 (Songweaver).  Resonant Types & Path Points →
  3. Choose your Paths. Distribute Path Points across the six lawful Paths. No single Path may exceed your per-Path cap — cap of 3 per Path for Hybrids, cap of 4 per Path for True Resonants.  The Six Lawful Paths →
  4. Apply Lineage Path Affinity. (+1 to the Path listed on your Lineage.) Hybrids: this bonus does not raise any single Path above 3.  Resonant Types & Path Points →
  5. Note your Governing Attribute for each Path you invested in. This feeds directly into your Capability formula.  Governing Attributes →
  6. Record your Wellspring Pool. Fixed by your Calling type (36 / 24 / 4 Cadence).  Determining Your Wellspring →
  7. Understand Cast resolution. Your Path Rating, Governing Attribute, and Wellspring all interact during play.  Resolving a Cast →
  8. Songweavers: You use the Cadence Discipline system, not Path-based Resonance. See the Songweaver Calling page.

Distributing Path Points

Distribute your Path Points freely across the six lawful Paths. You may concentrate all points in one Path or spread them across several.

  • Specialization — Deep investment in one or two Paths increases precision and power within those harmonics.
  • Versatility — Spreading points across multiple Paths increases your options at the cost of depth.
  • Both approaches are lawful. The choice defines your identity as a practitioner.
Hybrid Cap: No individual Path Rating may exceed 3 per Path. Lineage Affinity bonuses do not raise this cap.
Lineage Path Affinity: Your Lineage grants +1 Path Affinity. Apply this after distributing your base Path Points. This bonus can raise a True Resonant's rating toward 4, but does not override the Hybrid cap of 3 per Path.

III

The Six Lawful Paths

These are the governing harmonics through which the Song expresses itself. A practitioner does not invent a Path — they align with it.

Each Path is defined by a First Law — an immutable governing principle from which all expression derives. Attempting to bend a First Law does not produce an unusual result. It produces resistance, misalignment, and failure.

Aeris

First Law: Displacement

Governing Attribute: Agility

The harmonic of survival through motion. Aeris favors reaction over preparation, repositioning over resistance, and momentum over mass. Where others brace, Aeris relocates.

Excels at: Speed, pursuit, escape, spatial disruption, momentum redirection.

Drift toward failure: Recklessness. Avoidance. Abandonment disguised as survival.

Aqua

First Law: Absorption

Governing Attribute: Vitality

Aqua survives by receiving rather than resisting. It carries strain inward and redistributes it rather than shattering. Stability through endurance.

Excels at: Pressure regulation, sustained resilience, emotional attunement, burden carrying.

Drift toward failure: Stagnation. Emotional suppression. Drowning in accumulated burden.

Ignis

First Law: Combustive Transformation

Governing Attribute: Intelligence

Ignis ends stalemates through escalation. It burns to reshape. Change through decisive ignition — it does not maintain, it transforms.

Excels at: Rapid alteration, catalytic breakthrough, decisive escalation.

Drift toward failure: Volatility. Destruction without purpose.

Obtenebrate

First Law: Perceptual Distortion

Governing Attribute: Agility

Obtenebrate alters awareness rather than substance. It wins before confrontation begins. Victory through misdirection — it does not dominate openly.

Excels at: Concealment, infiltration, perceptual manipulation, subtle redirection.

Drift toward failure: Manipulation without purpose. Deception as identity.

Terra

First Law: Refusal through Solidity

Governing Attribute: Strength

Terra survives by refusing displacement. It meets force directly and denies motion. Security through immovability — it does not flow or adapt rapidly.

Excels at: Fortification, anchoring, structural reinforcement, sustained resistance.

Drift toward failure: Rigidity. Obstruction. Inflexible dominance.

Vitae

First Law: Adaptive Growth

Governing Attribute: Wisdom

Vitae preserves through refinement. It stabilizes and strengthens what lives. Preservation through adaptive correction — it cannot weaponize the life it tends.

Excels at: Healing, reinforcement, regenerative stabilization, biological correction.

Drift toward failure: Overgrowth. Control disguised as care.

What Resonance Cannot Do

  • Vitae cannot kill. It preserves and adapts — it does not weaponize life.
  • Aeris cannot anchor. It displaces — it does not lock.
  • Terra cannot flow. It refuses — it does not redirect.
  • Ignis cannot preserve. It transforms — it does not maintain.
  • Aqua cannot erase what it absorbs. It carries burden — it does not annihilate.
  • Obtenebrate cannot overpower through open force. It misdirects — it does not dominate.
  • Resonance may not instantly unmake living internal structures. The world protects its living patterns.
IV

Determining Your Wellspring

Your Wellspring Pool is the total volume of Resonant energy your mind and spirit can navigate before focus shatters. This is determined by your Calling, not your choices.

Wellspring Formula

Standard Formula

Total WP = Die Value + (Die Value × 2)

The die value is taken as the maximum value of the Wellspring Die.

Wellspring Reference

Calling Type Wellspring Die Calculation Total WP
True Resonant d12 12 + (12 × 2) 36 WP
Hybrid d8 8 + (8 × 2) 24 WP
Songweaver Cadence Fixed starting pool 4 Cadence Pts
Note: Wellspring is fixed at creation — it reflects the maximum capacity of your Calling's connection to the Song, not variance.
V

Resolving a Cast

When you instruct the Song during play, resolution follows a unified model. Understanding this at creation will shape how you invest your Path Points and Primary Ratings.

What Is Resonance?

Resonance is not spellcasting. It is not prayer. It is the structured instruction of the Song — a pervasive lattice woven through all matter, motion, and breath within Rivendawn. The Song does not think. It does not judge. It responds. A Resonant practitioner does not cast — they instruct. And the Song obeys, within law.

Three Conditions of Every Cast

  • Clarity of Instruction — The Song answers structured will, not vague desire.
  • Magnitude of Disruption — The greater the alteration, the greater the resistance.
  • Cost of Sustenance — The Wellspring is finite. No instruction is without burden.

Capability Formula

Your Capability

Capability = Primary Attribute + Ability or Knowledge + Path Rating

Target Number

Target Number = Baseline − Capability

Roll 1d20. Meet or exceed the Target Number to succeed.

Magnitude Tiers

Scope of the Cast determines difficulty, not cost. Three tiers are defined:

Standard

No Modifier

Single target or contained effect

Expanded

+3 TN

Multiple targets or larger structural alteration

Mass

+9 TN

Battlefield scale or large environmental manipulation

Resonance Cost per Cast

Cost is flat per Cast. Failure costs more — harmonic invocation occurred without resolution. Energy was engaged but not harmonized.

5 RP True Resonant — Successful Cast
8 RP True Resonant — Failed Cast
7 RP Hybrid — Successful Cast
9 RP Hybrid — Failed Cast

Special Resolutions

  • Natural 1 — Harmonic Inversion: The declared effect manifests upon the caster instead of the intended target. If declared at Expanded or Mass scope, the backlash reflects that magnitude. The Song does not punish — it mirrors.
  • Natural 20 — Ultimate Success: The Cast succeeds regardless of Target Number. Additional effects are defined on your Calling sheet.

A Cast Is a Single Expression

A Cast cannot bundle multiple independent effects. The declaration must identify specific intent, scope, and target. If multiple discrete outcomes are desired, they require multiple Casts. Resonance is applied, not stacked.
