Steward

The Steward understood power more clearly than those who held it: that a domain only endures when someone is watching the grain stores, reading the labor costs, and quietly ensuring that the soil, the people, and the future remain viable. Before the Calling, they kept entire worlds turning behind the scenes — ledgers by lamplight, dawn walks through orchards, the constant, unglamorous arithmetic of keeping people fed and lands alive.

Imprinted in Tactical & Martial, the Steward arrives at their Calling with a practiced mastery of long-horizon strategy — resource allocation, command presence, the ability to hold complex systems together under pressure — a skillset that turns potential chaos into sustained order and makes them invaluable far beyond any single battlefield.

You are the Steward — you understood power more clearly than those who held it. A domain only endures when someone is watching the grain stores, reading the labor costs, and quietly ensuring that the soil, the people, and the future remain viable. You kept entire worlds turning behind the scenes.

Heritage Imprint

◆

This Vocation imprints Tactical & Martial because stewardship is the art of long-term strategy applied to land and people — anticipating shortages, allocating resources, commanding labor without force, maintaining order so the whole system doesn't collapse. It is the deep knowing of how to hold a household or estate like a battlefield won without bloodshed, turning potential chaos into sustained harmony through planning and quiet authority.

Locked Heritage Rating

4 Tactical & Martial
"Practiced" Mastery Level

You still sense when resources are thinning, still know how to organize people without breaking them, still carry the calm certainty of someone who once kept an entire world turning smoothly behind the scenes.

Selectable Skills

Choose two from the four below

Knowledge Economics & Trade

Crop yields, market prices, taxes, labor costs, trade routes — the knowledge that turned potential ruin into surplus, ensuring the land and its people could thrive instead of merely survive.

Knowledge Insight

Who to trust, who to push, who needs help before they ask — the quiet reading of hearts and motives that kept labor loyal, tenants honest, and lords satisfied without force.

Ability Tactics

Positioning workers, timing harvests, allocating supplies, foreseeing shortages — running land is tactical warfare without swords, and the skill that let you outmaneuver drought, debt, and discontent before they became crises.

Ability Command

Directing laborers, negotiating with merchants, guiding lords toward wise choices — the presence and clarity that made people follow willingly, turning a household into a living, breathing system.
