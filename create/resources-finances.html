Resources & Finances

Material Foundation
Courage, knowledge, and Resonance define what a character can accomplish. But practical realities shape how they move through the world. Equipment must be purchased. Doors sometimes open only to those with the right connections. Survival often depends as much on preparation as it does on skill.
The Golden Rule of Acquisition Resources and Financial Standing are purchased exclusively during Character Creation using Freebie Points (FP). They cannot be subsidized by Liabilities, traded for Skill Points, or generated by any other means. They represent the character's permanent starting foundation — the material and social baseline from which they enter the world.

The Currency — Echo-Vane

The economy of Rivendawn is anchored by physical, refined Echo-Vane currency — valued not only for its stability but for the faint resonance it carries within the world's deeper structure.

◈
Tarnished Bit
TB — Base Unit
Raw, low-purity shavings used for micro-transactions. The currency of the desperate and the destitute.
100 TB = 1 Mark
⬡
The Mark
MK — Standard Trade Unit
High-purity hexagonal coin. The standard of commerce across all three regions and both Citadels. Bears the seal of its originating mint on the reverse.

Social & Material Resources

Non-monetary advantages possessed at the start of play. Creation cap: Rank 3. Click any category to expand its rank descriptions.

Informant Network ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
A few street-level lookouts or a single reliable clerk. Useful for local rumors and basic surveillance.
Rank 2 2 FP
A dedicated cell of spies with reliable communication channels and access to mid-level information.
Rank 3 5 FP
A "Spider's Web" within the city's inner sanctums. May generate one significant lead per session within that city. Additional use requires Watcher escalation.
Benefactor ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
A low-level official, veteran, or minor merchant with limited but genuine influence in their sphere.
Rank 2 2 FP
A merchant prince or guild leader with regional reach and resources to deploy on your behalf.
Rank 3 5 FP
A High-Watcher or Noble patron with the power to intervene at significant political or military levels.
Underworld Contacts ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
A reliable fence for illicit Echo-Vane transactions and basic criminal services.
Rank 2 2 FP
Access to black-market stimulants, restricted gear, and a network of mid-level criminal operators.
Rank 3 5 FP
Membership within a high-ranking syndicate with organizational backing, protection, and serious illicit resources.
Emergency Cache ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
A hidden locker with basic supplies and 10 Marks. Enough to get out of a tight spot.
Rank 2 2 FP
A safehouse containing medical kits, tools, and a modest reserve of equipment.
Rank 3 5 FP
Three fortified safehouses distributed across the region, each stocked and maintained.
Legal Immunity ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
Immunity for minor infractions within a defined jurisdiction.
Rank 2 2 FP
Guards must succeed on a contested Authority or Command check to conduct a search under standard conditions.
Rank 3 5 FP
A High-Watcher's Seal preventing arrest for minor and standard infractions within the issuing region. Does not override capital crimes, military jurisdiction, or direct Watcher intervention.
Region Renown ▼
Rank 1 1 FP
People have heard of you. Your name carries minor weight in local conversations.
Rank 2 2 FP
Citizens across the region recognize your name and reputation on sight.
Rank 3 5 FP
"No one dares mess with you." Hostile NPCs must succeed on a Social or Command-based check (TN set by the Watcher) to initiate aggression within your home region.
Creation Cap: No Resource category may exceed Rank 3 at character creation. Resources reflect your starting position — not your potential. What you build from here is the story.
