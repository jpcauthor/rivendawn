Identity — Contract Over Creed

The Mercenary studies battlefield economics, engagement probability, armor failure points, and withdrawal timing. They understand that most causes collapse, most banners burn, and most ideals fade. Coin endures. So does reputation. A Mercenary does not fight recklessly — they calculate risk, mitigate exposure, and leave the field alive enough to take the next job. Their relationship with Resonance is practical: they use it if it helps, ignore it if it doesn't. Power is a tool, not a creed.

Skill Matrix

AbilityWeapon Mastery

The Mercenary adapts to whatever tool the contract demands — trained broadly across blades, axes, and ranged implements. Versatility keeps employment steady.

AbilityTactics

A Mercenary survives by understanding positioning, threat hierarchy, and retreat lanes. They do not charge blindly — they exploit weakness and disengage when margins shift.

AbilityEndurance

Campaigns are long. Sieges are longer. The Mercenary must remain effective after exhaustion sets in, standing when lesser soldiers collapse.

KnowledgeEconomics & Trade

A Mercenary knows the value of risk, supply lines, armor quality, and negotiated rates. Understanding trade allows them to secure better contracts and avoid exploitative ones.

Bestowed Gifts of the Calling

Defensive Gift

Hardened Kit

The Mercenary gains an additional 10 Armor Points, representing layered reinforcement, experience-driven padding of critical zones, and practical battlefield modifications.

These points reflect preparation, not mysticism. The Mercenary survives because they expect to be hit.

Offensive Gift

Calculated Exploit

When the Mercenary rolls an Ultimate Success, roll 1d4:

  • Odd result: Target's TN becomes 13 for one turn.
  • Even result: Target's TN becomes 13 for two turns.

This represents targeted disruption of stance, breath, or guard. The Mercenary does not waste opportunity — they capitalize on it.

Combat Surges

Offensive Surge

Contract Fulfilled

The Mercenary commits to a decisive strike intended to end the engagement quickly and efficiently.

Trigger — Declare before making an attack

  • Gain +4 Offensive Rating for the attack.
  • If the attack hits, it deals +50% damage.

The Mercenary commits fully to the strike, finishing the job before the battlefield turns against them.

Defensive Surge

Tactical Withdrawal

The Mercenary disengages from danger when the battlefield shifts against them. May be declared after being targeted — but cannot be used while stunned, disoriented, unconscious, or surprised.

Trigger — When targeted by an attack

Immediately move up to 4 meters. If the attacker cannot reach your new position, the attack automatically misses.

The Mercenary steps out of the engagement line at the last possible moment, choosing survival over stubbornness.

Playstyle Hook

You fight for the contract, not the cause. You reinforce your armor, read the field, and exploit weakness when it appears. You are neither zealot nor martyr. You are the professional who walks away when others fall. Reputation is currency. Survival is proof of competence.
