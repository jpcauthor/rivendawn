The Vanguard

Among the institutions associated with the Citadels, few have held greater responsibility than the order known as the Vanguard. Established during the early centuries of the Citadel’s existence, the Vanguard was created to serve as the guardians and protectors of the Citadel and its archives.

While the Citadel itself was devoted to scholarship, its founders recognized that knowledge alone could not guarantee safety. The archives contained information of great value and, in some cases, considerable danger. Protecting these records—and the scholars who maintained them—required individuals capable of discipline, skill, and unwavering loyalty to the Citadel’s mission.

The Vanguard was formed to fulfill this role.

Members of the Vanguard were selected for their exceptional ability and character. Many possessed extensive training in martial disciplines, while others were chosen for their experience navigating dangerous territories beyond the Citadel’s walls. Regardless of their origin, all were bound by a shared oath to protect the Citadel, its scholars, and the knowledge entrusted to its care.

The duties of the Vanguard extended beyond simple defense. Members of the order frequently escorted Citadel expeditions into distant regions, safeguarded scholars traveling across Rivendawn, and assisted in the recovery of historical records from remote settlements. In this way, the Vanguard played a vital role in the expansion of the Citadel’s archives and the preservation of knowledge throughout the world.

For centuries, the Vanguard maintained a reputation for discipline and neutrality. Their loyalty was directed not toward any individual leader but toward the ideals upon which the Citadel had been founded. As long as the institution remained united, the Vanguard functioned as a stabilizing force that helped ensure the safety of its scholars and the integrity of its work.

This unity was tested during the events of the Citadel Schism.

The killing of Lorn Zyreth by Greighton Lastlight placed the Vanguard in an unprecedented position. When the Citadel’s leadership declared Greighton a traitor, members of the Vanguard were forced to confront a difficult choice between obedience to authority and loyalty to their own interpretation of the Citadel’s mission.

The resulting division fractured the order. Some members remained aligned with the leadership of the Crimson Citadel, while others believed Greighton’s actions had been justified. This conflict, sometimes referred to as the Internal Vanguard War, permanently ended the unity that had defined the order for generations.

In the years that followed, many Vanguard members continued to serve within the Crimson Citadel. Others, however, chose to support the founding of the Azure Citadel, where they now act as protectors of its scholars and archives.

As a result, the Vanguard no longer exists as a single unified order. Instead, its legacy continues through individuals and groups aligned with the institutions they now serve.

Despite this division, the reputation of the Vanguard endures. Across Rivendawn, the name still carries associations of discipline, courage, and a long tradition of service dedicated to the protection of knowledge.
