The Citadels and the Regions

Although the Citadels were founded as institutions devoted to scholarship rather than political rule, their presence has long influenced the broader landscape of Rivendawn. Over the centuries, the relationship between the Citadels and the major regions of the world has developed through a combination of cooperation, tension, and mutual dependence.

For much of recorded history, the Citadel maintained a position of deliberate neutrality. Its scholars sought to preserve knowledge and study Resonance without becoming directly involved in the governance of the surrounding lands. This stance allowed the institution to serve as a gathering place for individuals from many cultures and traditions, each contributing to the Citadel’s growing body of knowledge.

At the same time, the regions of Rivendawn recognized the value of the Citadel’s archives and instruction. Students from distant settlements traveled to study within its halls, and regional leaders occasionally consulted Citadel scholars regarding matters of history, language, or Resonance. In this way, the Citadel developed a reputation as an intellectual authority even while avoiding formal political control.

As the regions of The Forge, The Cradle, and Limestone matured during the Age of Regions and the Citadel Plateau, their interactions with the Citadel gradually became more complex.

The Forge has traditionally valued strength, discipline, and practical skill. While its leaders respect the usefulness of knowledge, they often view the Citadel through the lens of strategic advantage, recognizing the importance of understanding Resonance while remaining cautious of outside influence.

The Cradle, by contrast, has historically maintained closer intellectual ties with the Citadel. Its traditions of governance and diplomacy align naturally with the study of law, language, and historical record. Scholars from the Cradle have long been among the most frequent contributors to the Citadel’s academic community.

Limestone maintains a more independent relationship with the Citadel. While its communities respect the value of learning and often send students to study within the Citadel’s halls, Limestone’s culture places greater emphasis on local responsibility and communal resilience than on centralized institutions.

The Citadel Schism introduced new complexities into these relationships. With the existence of both the Crimson and Azure Citadels, the regions of Rivendawn now find themselves interacting with two rival institutions that each claim to uphold the legacy of the original Citadel.

Different communities and leaders respond to this division in different ways. Some continue to maintain relationships with the Crimson Citadel due to its long-established authority and extensive archives. Others view the Azure Citadel as a continuation of the principles that once guided the institution before the Schism.

As a result, the balance of influence between the Citadels and the regions remains an evolving matter. While the Citadels continue to function primarily as centers of learning, their existence inevitably shapes the intellectual and political currents of the world.

Understanding these relationships is essential for students seeking to comprehend the modern structure of Rivendawn, where knowledge, power, and tradition often intersect in complex ways.

With the role of the Citadels understood, the study of Rivendawn may now turn to the lands and societies that form the physical and cultural foundation of the world: the Regions of Rivendawn.
