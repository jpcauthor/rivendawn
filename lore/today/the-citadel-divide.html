The Citadel Divide

For much of Rivendawn’s recorded history, the Citadel stood as the central institution of learning, governance, and the disciplined study of Resonance. Its archives preserved knowledge from across the world, and its scholars trained generations of individuals capable of understanding and interacting with the Song.

This stability was disrupted by events that would later be known collectively as the Citadel Schism.

The origins of the divide can be traced to internal conflicts concerning the study and application of Resonance—particularly the growing concern surrounding experimental practices involving dangerous and poorly understood forms of Resonant manipulation. As debate intensified within the Citadel, disagreements that had once been academic began to take on political and ideological significance.

These tensions ultimately culminated in a violent conflict within the Citadel itself. The struggle involved members of the Vanguard and several influential scholars whose differing views on the control and study of Resonance could no longer be reconciled. The conflict ended with the consolidation of power within the Crimson Citadel under the authority of Dominus Scientiae Grevon.

Under Grevon’s leadership, the Crimson Citadel adopted a far more rigid and centralized structure than the institution had previously maintained. Access to knowledge became increasingly restricted, and the training of Resonant practitioners was brought firmly under Citadel authority.

Not all members of the Citadel accepted these changes.

A group of scholars, instructors, and Resonant practitioners chose to separate from the Crimson Citadel, believing that the institution had abandoned the principles upon which it was originally founded. These individuals established a new center of study that came to be known as the Azure Citadel.

The Azure Citadel seeks to preserve the traditions of open scholarship and ethical responsibility that its founders believe once defined the original Citadel system. However, its resources and influence remain limited when compared to the established authority of the Crimson Citadel.

The result is a world in which two institutions now claim to represent the legitimate continuation of the Citadel tradition.

Despite the tension between them, the divide has not yet escalated into open warfare. Instead, the relationship between the Citadels is best described as one of ideological rivalry, political maneuvering, and cautious observation.

Both institutions continue to train Resonant practitioners, preserve knowledge, and exert influence across Rivendawn. Yet each does so according to its own interpretation of what the study of the Song should represent.

For the peoples of Rivendawn, the existence of two Citadels raises questions that scholars and rulers alike continue to debate.

If knowledge is power, who should control it?

And which institution now speaks with the true authority of the Citadel tradition?
