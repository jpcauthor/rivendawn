Introduction — The Present Age

The world of Rivendawn now stands in a time historians increasingly refer to as the Present Age. Unlike the earlier centuries following the founding of the Citadel—when civilization struggled simply to survive and rebuild—this era is defined by growing complexity, rising political tension, and a widening uncertainty about the future.

For nearly eight centuries after the establishment of the first Citadel, the world experienced gradual recovery. Writing returned. Trade routes expanded. Settlements grew into organized regions capable of sustaining stable populations. Institutions dedicated to the study of Resonance brought new understanding to forces that had once been feared as mysterious or uncontrollable.

Yet stability has never meant permanence.

In recent years, events within the Citadel itself have shaken the foundations of the institutions that once served as the central anchor of knowledge and governance. The conflict that resulted—now commonly referred to as the Citadel Schism—has altered the balance of power across Rivendawn.

At the heart of this division lies the transformation of the Crimson Citadel, once the primary guardian of history and scholarship. Under the authority of the Dominus Scientiae, Grevon, the Citadel has grown increasingly authoritarian in its control over knowledge, Resonant training, and political influence.

Opposing this development is the newly established Azure Citadel, formed by those who rejected the growing corruption within the old institution. Though still young and far smaller in number, the Azure Citadel represents an effort to preserve the original ideals upon which the Citadel system was founded.

Beyond the Citadels, the regions of Rivendawn have matured into powerful societies in their own right. The disciplined industrial strength of the Forge, the structured governance and scholarship of the Cradle, and the resilient communal traditions of Limestone each represent distinct approaches to building civilization in a world still shaped by the lingering consequences of the Resonant Pulse.

These powers coexist in a state of cautious equilibrium. Alliances exist, but they are often informal and fragile. Old conflicts remain remembered, and new tensions continue to emerge as societies expand their influence and pursue their own visions for the future of Rivendawn.

Complicating this landscape are reports of increasingly unstable Resonant phenomena, the lingering threat of Shadowmancy, and the political consequences of the Citadel’s internal fracture. Together, these developments have created an atmosphere in which many observers believe the world may soon enter another period of significant transformation.

For students studying the present age, one conclusion becomes clear: Rivendawn is no longer a world struggling merely to survive. It is a world beginning to decide what kind of civilization it intends to become.

And that decision remains far from settled.
