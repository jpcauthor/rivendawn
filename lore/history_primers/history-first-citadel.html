The Founding of the First Red Citadel (0 RC)

The beginning of recorded history in Rivendawn is marked by the founding of the First Red Citadel, an institution established for the preservation and advancement of knowledge among the peoples of the world.

By the time of the Citadel’s founding, the lands of Rivendawn were inhabited by many distinct cultures and lineages. These peoples possessed their own traditions, beliefs, and survival practices, yet few maintained systems capable of preserving knowledge beyond the span of a single generation. Oral histories served as the primary means of transmitting memory, but such traditions often changed with time, and important truths were easily lost.

Recognizing the danger posed by the gradual erosion of knowledge, two scholars—Blanca Whitestorm and Granger Oathsworn—undertook the effort to establish a permanent institution dedicated to the preservation of learning. Drawing upon surviving fragments of earlier texts and traditions, they envisioned a place where knowledge could be gathered, recorded, and protected from the uncertainties of the wider world.

The result of this effort was the founding of the First Red Citadel.

The Citadel was conceived not as the seat of a kingdom, but as a scholarly institution open to all peoples willing to pursue the disciplined study of knowledge. Its mandate was fourfold: to preserve surviving records of the past, to protect dangerous knowledge from misuse, to train individuals capable of understanding the growing phenomenon of Resonance, and to restore the written word as a reliable means of preserving history.

From the Citadel’s earliest days, scribes and archivists were dispatched across the surrounding lands to gather surviving accounts from travelers, elders, and scattered communities. These efforts formed the foundation of the Citadel Archives, which continue to expand as new discoveries are made.

The founding of the Citadel also marked the beginning of formal instruction. Students from various lineages began to travel to the Citadel to study language, history, philosophy, and the emerging field of Resonance scholarship. Through this exchange of knowledge, the Citadel became one of the first institutions in recorded history where individuals from many cultures gathered for a shared purpose.

Although the Citadel did not seek political authority, its influence grew steadily as the centuries passed. Communities across Rivendawn came to recognize the value of a neutral institution devoted to preserving knowledge and documenting the events of the wider world.

For historians, the significance of the First Red Citadel lies not merely in the structure itself, but in what it represents. Its founding restored the ability of societies to record their experiences, preserve their discoveries, and transmit knowledge beyond the limits of memory.

With the Citadel’s establishment, the historical age of Rivendawn truly began. The centuries that followed would see the gradual spread of settlements, the development of cultures and institutions, and the slow emergence of the civilizations that shape the world today.

The earliest of these centuries are known collectively as the Founding Age, the first of the great historical eras recorded by the Citadel.
