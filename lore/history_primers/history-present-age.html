The Present Age (870–877 RC)

The years following the Citadel Schism are known collectively as The Present Age, a period defined by uncertainty, shifting alliances, and the continued consequences of the events that fractured the Citadel less than a decade ago.

For much of recorded history, the Citadel had served as a singular institution responsible for preserving knowledge and guiding the study of Resonance. With the emergence of two rival Citadels—the Crimson Citadel under the leadership of Grevon, and the newly established Azure Citadel led by Veysha—that long-standing unity has been replaced by a more complex and fragile balance of influence.

The Crimson Citadel continues to occupy its historic seat of authority and retains control of much of the institutional structure built during the Citadel Plateau. Its archives, scholars, and political influence remain considerable, and many still view it as the primary center of learning in Rivendawn. Under Grevon’s leadership, however, the institution has taken on a more centralized and assertive role in the governance of knowledge and the regulation of Resonance studies.

The Azure Citadel, though far younger, has rapidly attracted scholars and students who believe the traditions of open inquiry and academic independence must be preserved. Supported by members of the surviving Vanguard and other allies sympathetic to its mission, the Azure Citadel has begun establishing its own archives, training programs, and scholarly networks.

Beyond the Citadels, the three major regions of Rivendawn—The Forge, The Cradle, and Limestone—continue to grow in strength and stability. Each region maintains its own cultural traditions and systems of governance, and each views the division of the Citadel through the lens of its own political priorities. While none of these regions currently seeks direct conflict with the others, the presence of two rival centers of scholarly authority has introduced new uncertainties into the broader political landscape.

At the same time, scholars across both Citadels have reported an increase in unusual Resonant disturbances throughout several parts of Rivendawn. Although the causes of these phenomena remain unclear, their growing frequency has renewed interest in the deeper study of the Song and its influence upon the world.

For the moment, Rivendawn exists in a delicate equilibrium. The regions remain stable, trade continues, and the institutions of learning still function—though no longer with the unity that once defined them. Beneath this surface stability, however, the tensions created by the Citadel Schism continue to shape the decisions of leaders, scholars, and travelers across the realm.

It is within this uncertain landscape that the present year—877 RC—unfolds.

The history of Rivendawn has reached a moment where the actions of individuals may once again influence the direction of the world. Whether the divisions of the present age will lead to renewed conflict or a new balance among the powers of Rivendawn remains a matter for the future to determine.

For students of history, the events of the Present Age offer a reminder that history is not merely the study of the past. It is the unfolding story of the world itself—one in which the present generation now plays its part.
