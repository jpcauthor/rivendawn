Dice Resolution System

Every uncertain action in Rivendawn is answered by a single question: does your will overcome the resistance of the world? The Dice Resolution System provides a fast, unified method for determining the outcome of every attempt, from quiet skill checks to decisive moments of combat and Resonance. One roll of the die decides success or failure, while ultimate outcomes and the deeper laws of the Song ensure that even the simplest roll can reshape the story.

Dice Resolution System

Heroes Resonance Core Doctrine

I. Universal Resolution Rule

All actions in Heroes Resonance use a unified subtractive capability model.

Target Number (TN) = Level Baseline – Capability

Roll 1d20.

  • If the roll is equal to or greater than the TN, the action succeeds.
  • If the roll is less than the TN, the action fails.

There are no partial successes. Binary resolution is intentional: clarity over granularity.

  • A natural 20 is an Ultimate Success.
  • A natural 1 is an Ultimate Failure, regardless of TN.

This structure produces predictable probability curves while reserving dramatic variance for the extremes.

II. Capability

Capability represents a character’s ability to successfully perform a specific action.

Capability = Attribute + Ability/Knowledge + Heritage Imprint

The Context Rating depends on the system being used.

Examples include:

  • Offensive Rating (martial combat)
  • Path Rating (Resonant instruction)
  • Heritage Imprint (vocational or experiential knowledge)

Each subsystem defines the specific rating that applies.

III. Level Baseline Table

Level represents maturation, adaptation, and a deepening relationship with the Song.

As heroes grow stronger, the world resists them less.

Level Baseline
1–2 19
3–4 18
5–6 17
7–8 16
9–10 15

The baseline decreases every two levels.

As heroes advance, tension shifts from:

“Can I succeed?”

to

“What will success cost?”

IV. Binary Success Doctrine

Resolution in Heroes Resonance is binary.

Actions either succeed or fail.

Magnitude, intensity, duration, and collateral effects are determined by other systems, including:

  • Resonant magnitude tiers
  • Damage dice
  • Wellspring expenditure
  • Ability/Knowledge relevance
  • Narrative consequences
  • Calling-specific Gifts

Success means the intended action occurs.

Failure means it does not.

V. Ultimate Outcomes

Ultimate outcomes represent moments where the Song responds in extreme harmony or dissonance.

These outcomes override the normal binary model.

Ultimate Success (Natural 20)

The action succeeds with exceptional resonance.

Specific effects are defined by abilities, Callings, and advanced system rules.

Ultimate Failure (Natural 1)

Failure occurs regardless of TN.

Additional consequences depend on the nature of the action.

Examples include:

Mundane Combat

  • Weapon fumble for one round
  • A full turn is required to regain control

Resonant Hybrid Action

  • Double Wellspring cost is lost
  • Resonant backlash applies the intended effect to the Hybrid for one round

Resonant Casting

  • Double Wellspring cost is lost
  • The caster cannot instruct from that Path on the following round

Ultimate failures preserve the importance of rhythm, focus, and disciplined instruction.

VI. Watcher Resolution

The Watcher resolves NPC and hostile actions using the same universal system.

TN = Baseline – Capability

Threat Tiers determine the baseline equivalent used by non-player forces.

This ensures:

  • Mechanical symmetry between heroes and adversaries
  • Predictable probability structures
  • No hidden advantages or disadvantages between player and Watcher resolution

VII. Design Philosophy

Heroes Resonance is a mythic campaign system.

Accuracy is not the primary axis of tension.

Heroes are expected to succeed often and decisively.

Meaningful tension arises from:

  • Wellspring attrition
  • Harmonic dissonance
  • Battlefield tempo
  • Tactical positioning
  • Risk versus reward
  • Ultimate outcomes
  • Narrative consequence
  • Resonant magnitude escalation

Victory is not landing a blow.

Victory is enduring the cost of acting.
