The Forge Lands

The Forge Lands occupy the western edge of the known world, where the terrain rises sharply and does not apologize for it. The Iron Peaks — a range of mountains whose upper reaches remain snow-covered through every season — form both the region's spine and its most effective defense. Passes through the peaks are narrow, treacherous, and watched. The Forge did not build walls around its territory because the mountains already did that work.

Below the peaks, the terrain descends through a series of rocky basins and high valleys where the earliest Forge communities took root. These basins are mineral-rich — iron, copper, coal, and stone in quantities that have sustained the region's industry for centuries. The land does not grow food easily here. What it grows instead is ore, and the Forge has built an entire civilization on that fact.

Much of Forge life exists underground or inside the mountain itself. The cavern systems carved by centuries of mining have become permanent settlements — fortified, heated by forge-fire and geothermal vents, and connected by tunnels that run deeper than most outsiders know. A traveler who enters the Forge Lands from the east sees mountains. What they do not see are the cities inside them.

The foundry halls are the beating heart of this territory. Here, ore pulled from the earth becomes the tools, armor, and weapons that the Forge trades throughout Rivendawn. The heat inside these spaces is relentless. The noise is constant. The people who work them are shaped by both — direct, enduring, and deeply skeptical of anyone who has not earned their place through demonstrated labor.

For travelers, the Forge Lands are unforgiving terrain that rewards preparation and punishes assumptions. The mountain passes are the only reliable entry points, and all of them are garrisoned. The weather at elevation can shift from difficult to lethal without warning. Supplies do not stretch as far here as they do in the lowlands, and the locals have little patience for those who arrive expecting hospitality. The Forge does not owe the world comfort. It offers trade, and it offers iron. Everything else must be earned.
